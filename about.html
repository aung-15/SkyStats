About the Project

Meet Team 2

SkyStats is a final project for DH 101 (Digital Humanities), Summer 2026, built by a six-person team exploring how COVID-19 reshaped commercial air travel in the United States.

Our Goal

Turning Federal Flight Data Into a Story

Millions of rows of federal flight-operations data can tell us a lot about how the aviation industry runs — but on their own, they don't tell a story. Our goal with SkyStats was to take the Bureau of Transportation Statistics' T-100 Segment dataset and turn it into a clear, honest account of what actually happened to American air travel during and after the COVID-19 pandemic: who stopped flying, who came back, and who still hasn't.

We focused on the ten largest U.S. metropolitan areas by air traffic volume — Atlanta, Chicago, Dallas–Fort Worth, Houston, Los Angeles, Miami, New York, Philadelphia, Phoenix, and Washington DC — and tracked monthly passengers, departures, and flight distances from 2019 through 2025.

How We Worked

Process & Collaboration

Our team communicated daily through a shared group chat and met regularly to check in on roles and milestones, storing all project files and drafts in a shared Google Drive. We divided labor across the roles above while staying flexible — several team members contributed to data work, writing, and design as the project evolved.

Decisions were made by majority rule with a strong preference for compromise, and we prioritized starting tasks early so there was always time left to review each other's work before deadlines.
